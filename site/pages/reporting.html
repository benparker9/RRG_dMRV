One Print One Tree Program (1P1T)

Reduce.Reuse.Grow (RRG) has partnered with Canva to plant a tree for every print made on their platform. This initiative, known as One Print, One Tree (1P1T), aims to restore degraded landscapes and sequester carbon through reforestation projects throughout Asia, Africa and South America.

Stakeholder Rights, Interests, and Relevance to the Project
Canva Project Financier - Funded through the One Print One Tree Program
Reduce Reuse Grow Inc RRG will serve as the project developer, providing technical guidance, project design, and overall coordination of activities. RRG manages monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) processes, ensures compliance with ERS standards, and will oversee the distribution of carbon credit revenues among stakeholders if we are to viably pursue VCUs under the isometric standard.
Friends of the Usambara FoU will implement on-the-ground activities including the development of project nurseries, land preparation, planting, and monitoring. They will manage the hiring of field employees, directly issue wages to laborers, and receive a portion of profits from carbon credit sales if realized.
Regional Governmental Councils Identified by RRG and the FoU through preliminary project mapping activities. The primary level of government to address is the Bumbuli District Council; they oversee regional land for project activities, then it funnels down to the ward and village level. These will be the main beneficiaries of carbon credit sales if realized as a project outcome.
EnviroWise EnviroWise is a leading environmental consulting firm based in Tanzania. RRG has contracted EnviroWise to assist with the FPIC process, including the drafting of documents and consulting both RRG and FoU in both the necessary steps and regulatory requirements for FPIC in Tanzania. Ultimately, this is a capacity-building exercise for RRG & FoU staff to manage future stakeholder consultation.
Local Community Members They will serve as field employees in project activities receiving stable wages in zones with limited income opportunities. They will also benefit from ecosystem services resulting from project implementation such as increased soil stability for landslide mitigation, more stable water supplies, and yields from the integration of agroforestry trees.

Canva Project Financier - Funded through the One Print One Tree Program (1P1T)
Reduce Reuse Grow Inc RRG will serve as the project developer, providing technical guidance, project design, and overall coordination of activities. RRG manages monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) processes and ensures compliance with ERS standards.
Dzalanyama Catchment Conservation Trust (DCCT) x MDOF DCCT and MDOF oversee the implementation of on-the-ground activities, including nursery development, land prep, planting, and firebreak maintenance. MDOF manages the hiring of field employees and issues wages to project laborers.
Local Community Members Planting is occurring on government owned plantation land contained within the boundaries of a Forest Reserve and locals are hired for field labor related to nursery work, land prep, planting, and maintenance. The DOF issues wages to laborers. The project is also a source of cleaner, cheaper charcoal that is not destructive to the region’s water supply.

Stakeholder Rights, Interests, and Relevance to the Project
Canva Project Financier - Funded through the One Print One Tree Program (1P1T)
Reduce Reuse Grow Inc RRG will serve as the project developer, providing technical guidance, project design, and overall coordination of activities. RRG manages monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) processes, ensures compliance with ERS standards, and will oversee the distribution of carbon credit revenues among stakeholders if we are to viably pursue VCUs under the isometric standard.
